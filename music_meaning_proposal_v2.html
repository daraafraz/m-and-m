04 · Design Rationale

Why This Direction

The Brief

The brief asked for a brand that feels intellectually serious without being academic, warm without being cosy, contemporary without chasing trends. The series draws on themes traced in the thought and music of Ostad Elahi and brings together performer-composers from different traditions; the identity aims to hold that depth and that openness without naming them literally. The concerts explore music and meaning — two words that resist reduction.

The Ampersand

Every logo in the shortlist treats the & differently — as interruption, as axis, as breathing space. The ampersand is where the two words meet; it's the most important glyph in the mark. In most directions it's italicised, lighter, and coloured in sage: a small softness held inside the structure.

The Rule

A thin 1pt rule appears throughout the system — flanking the ampersand in some marks, as a section divider in print, as a structural element in headers. It's the visual equivalent of a held breath: a pause that makes the silence meaningful.

The Palette

One palette for the series: paper, forest, ink. Not black-and-white; not heritage pastels. The forest ground comes from the idea of acoustic performance spaces — the aged timber of concert halls, the dark patina of pianos. Each of the six concerts takes a single accent from the same natural family (sage, ochre, umber, moss, stone, forest), so the system stays coherent while every programme has its own character.
